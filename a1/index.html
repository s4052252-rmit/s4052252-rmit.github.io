Interactive Experience Research Project

Interactive Experience Research Project
ADVENT CALENDER - LIKE
- WEB PORTFOLIO
by MYOUNGAE KIM
This research project about Advent Calendar-like Web Portfolio, an interactive website that transforms a design portfolio into a playful experience. Using the metaphor of an advent calendar, the work hidden projects within numbered boxes and festive 3D icons that respond to hovering, clicking, and dragging.

01

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The very first thing I noticed was the grid of numbered boxes displayed like an advent calendar. This immediately made me curious, as each box felt like a blind box hiding a surprise. The Christmas-inspired 3D icons caught my eye with their playful design, and the calendar motif gave me a sense of anticipation.

02

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

In two minutes, I hovered over every boxes and clicked on all different numbers to not to miss anything in this interactive project. Also read the short texts project description on the side. or viewed images that appeared. The one that impress me is that I can click and grab these graphic out side the box and put it anywhere on the website. and i can change thewebsite color to hot pink

03

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time clicking and opening the numbered boxes to see what content was hidden inside. Each one felt like a little blind boxes, so I focused on clicking on each boxes to see what inside.

04

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The action I repeated most often was hovering over the 3D Christmas elements to see them animate, then dragging them around and clicking to open and close the boxes. This became a playful rhythm: hover, click, reveal, and move.

05

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experiences?

The experience is designed to present the author portfolio in a festive, game-like way. Its primary goal seems to be encouraging curiosity and engagement by framing design projects as daily surprises, similar to how an advent calendar builds excitement and discovery.

06

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experiences?

The calendar grid, interactive icons, and hidden text clearly communicate the goal of exploration. When you click, the revealed paragraphs describe the designer’s inspirations (readings, movies, minimalism, maximalism, typography).

07

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

This feels like an experience you can explore in different ways: either all at once, by opening every box in one sitting, or gradually, returning multiple times to discover something new. Like a real advent calendar, it suggests pacing but doesn’t restrict you.

08

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The numbered layout suggests a slow reveal, yet the open-ended interaction design makes it flexible. The designer leaves the choice up to the user, encouraging repeated visits while also allowing for one-time, full exploration.

09

What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The design references physical advent calendars, Christmas decorations (trees, wreaths, ornaments), and concepts from design theory such as minimalism, maximalism, grid systems, and typography. It also echoes digital forms like interactive art books and online galleries.

10

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

These references tell me to act with curiosity and playfulness. The advent calendar metaphor suggests clicking around like opening gifts, without a fixed order, encouraging exploration and discovery.

11

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The references create a festive, joyful, and curious atmosphere. The Christmas visuals make the interaction feel friendly, while the embedded design concepts remind me I'm also learning about serious creative works.

12

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating aspect was that some boxes felt confusing when clicking on, take time to find out which boxes open or not. After the initial excitement, I wanted more depth and variety in what each element revealed and I want some thing more depth than that. Occasionally, the anticipation built by the design wasn’t fully matched by the reward

13

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part was the surprise of discovery. Each click revealed something new, whether a project, a design concept, or a whimsical animation. The combination of festive graphics, playful mechanics, and meaningful content turned the portfolio into a game-like exploration that kept me engaged.
